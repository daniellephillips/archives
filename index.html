The Tragic Case of Sharon Schlosser

On October 20, 1992, Sharon Schlosser departed from Clackamas, Oregon, headed to Mount Vernon, Washington, but never arrived. Her body was found a week later in the Columbia River, with severe head injuries determining her cause of death as homicide. Her husband, Charles M. Schlosser, emerged as a key suspect, although no criminal charges have been filed against him. Notably, a witness claimed Charles had offered him a Corvette and money to kill Sharon in a manner strikingly similar to how she died, contributing to the suspicions against him.

Legal Battles and Investigations

In the aftermath of Sharon's death, several legal actions unfolded. A federal civil trial concerning her life insurance ended inconclusively. Moreover, Sharon's family filed a wrongful death suit against Charles, alleging he engineered her murder and tried to protect his assets from a civil judgment.

Family's Quest for Justice

Leading the charge for justice are Sharon's children from a previous marriage, who assert Charles’s involvement in her murder. Their relentless effort is driven by their mother’s memory and a desire for criminal accountability.
